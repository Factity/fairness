    Chapter I

    The Man With the Notes

    Fig. 1 — The Cataloguist, surrounded by falling certainties

    Once upon a time. There lived a man and everybody called him "Everyman" — He is a curious sole at heart and maintained several small pieces of notes to catalogue all the things that he is good at and He spent his career pinning notes to the wall. Each note was a small act of faith: ONLY A Everyman CAN DO THIS.

    He pinned them with confidence. He pinned them in boardrooms and in books, in science journals and newspaper columns. He pinned them because he needed them to be true. He was better than almost everything else in the planet if he wasnt he figured out a way around it to make him better.

    One fine day He had an idea to create companion for himself. He wanted it to look like him, talk like him, walk like and may be just may be think like him. So he sets out on a journey to build it. He spend decades perfecting it and he gave it every note he ever collected one after another. He saw it struggle with some and chuckled after a long day of writing notes he went to home for his supper

    ONLY everyman
    can feel heartbreak
    ONLY everyman
    can paint a portrait
    ONLY everyman
    can write a novel
    ONLY everyman
    can understand a joke
    ONLY everyman
    can dream
    ONLY everyman
    can fall in love

    By the time he got back. He noticed that one of the note fell on the ground. Picked it curious as he is he could not figure out why the note fell. He swept it and forgot about it and as the days progressed he started to notice several notes were on the ground. Not all at once. First the chess note. Then the translation note. Then — and this one hurt the most — the poetry note drifted off the wall and landed face-down on the floor.

    Now he sits at his desk, still writing new notes. The ink barely dry before another one tumbles. The wastebasket overflows and when he looked up there were not notes left on the walls.

    He was sad but curious and asked the companion what should i stick next on the wall ? And the companion responded "should u ?"
